# SORA (空) — Sushi Brand Identity Guide

> *"Taste the sky"*

---

## 1. Brand Overview

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Name** | SORA (空) — "Sky" in Japanese |
| **Tagline** | Taste the sky |
| **Vibe** | Premium casual — high-end quality without pretension |
| **Tone** | Clean, serene, confident, modern Japanese | 
| **Target** | Food lovers, date nights, young professionals |

SORA is universal. It works anywhere sushi is enjoyed — from Ljubljana to London, Tokyo to Toronto. The name evokes lightness, freshness, and elevation — exactly what great sushi should deliver.

## 2. Color Palette

```
▮▮▮▮▮ #0A1628  Deep Navy    — Primary brand color
▮▮▮▮▮ #F5EFE6  Soft Cream   — Background, menus, packaging
▮▮▮▮▮ #FF6B6B  Coral        — Accent, CTAs, highlights
▮▮▮▮▮ #C4A35A  Gold         — Premium accent, logo details
▮▮▮▮▮ #2D2D2D  Charcoal     — Body text
▮▮▮▮▮ #FFFFFF  White        — Clean backgrounds
```

### Usage Rules
- **Navy** = primary for logo, headers, exterior signage
- **Cream** = menu backgrounds, packaging interiors, website backgrounds
- **Coral** = sparingly — buttons, specials markers, social media accents
- **Gold** = logo mark, premium callouts, awards/badges

---

## 3. Typography

### Primary: **Tinos** (serif)
- Use for logo wordmark and headlines
- Elegant, timeless, cross-cultural feel

### Secondary: **Inter** (sans-serif)
- Use for body text, menus, web copy
- Clean, highly legible, modern

### Tertiary: **Noto Sans JP** (Japanese glyphs)
- Use for the kanji 空 in logo variations
- Authentic Japanese character rendering

### Hierarchy
```
HEADLINE — Tinos Bold, 48/36/24pt
Subhead — Tinos Regular Italic, 20/18pt
Body — Inter Regular, 16/14pt
Small — Inter Medium, 12pt (menus, footnotes)
Accent — Noto Sans JP (kanji only)
```

---

## 4. Logo System

### Primary Logo
- **SORA** wordmark in Tinos Bold
- Small **空** kanji accent above or beside
- A subtle wave/crescent arch under the wordmark
- Colors: Navy on cream, or Gold on navy

### Secondary Logo (Stacked)
- **SORA** on one line
- **空** centered below
- "TASTE THE SKY" tagline below that in small caps

### Icon Mark
- Abstract wave/moon silhouette
- Monochrome (navy, gold, or white)
- Use as favicon, social avatar, stamp

### Clear Space
- Minimum: height of the "S" letter on all sides
- Never rotate, stretch, or apply effects

---

## 5. Brand Patterns & Textures

### Pattern 1: Wave (波浪)
- Linear repeating wave motif in cream or gold
- Derived from the Japanese sea/sky connection
- Use: packaging wrap, website backgrounds, napkins

### Pattern 2: Scale (鱗)
- Subtle hexagonal/fish-scale repeating pattern
- Use: takeout boxes, chopstick wrappers, wallpaper

### Texture: Washi Paper
- Use for menu cards, business cards, gift certificates
- Physical: off-white textured uncoated stock
- Digital: subtle grain overlay

---

## 6. Photography Style

### Food Photography
- **Lighting:** Soft, natural, side-lit — never harsh flash
- **Composition:** Minimal, negative space, overhead or 45° angle
- **Props:** Dark slate, light wood, bamboo mat, ceramic — no plastic
- **Color temp:** Warm (think golden hour) — not clinical white

### Lifestyle / Ambience
- **Palette:** Navy + warm wood + candlelight
- **Mood:** Intimate, focused, serene
- **People:** Hands preparing sushi, satisfied guests, chef portraits in dim light

---

## 7. Voice & Copywriting

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Short, declarative sentences | Corporate jargon |
| Evocative — "Fresh. Fast. Sky-high." | Generic — "Best sushi in town" |
| Confident but warm | Arrogant or pushy |
| Minimal punctuation | Exclamation marks everywhere |

### Sample Copy
- *"Sky's the limit. Every bite."*
- *"Fresh from the sea. Fast to your table."*
- *"SORA — taste the sky."*
- *"Simple ingredients. Elevated experience."*

## 8. Applications

### Menu
- Cream background, navy section headers
- Items grouped: Cold / Hot / Signature / Dessert / Drinks
- Gold dots as bullet markers, coral for chef's specials
- QR code in gold circle at bottom

### Takeout Packaging
- Navy box with gold foil **SORA** stamp
- Cream chopstick wrapper with wave pattern
- Coral wasabi/gari containers (pop of color inside the monochrome box)
- Minimal — no crowded text or stock photos

### Social Media (Instagram)
- **Grid pattern:** Every 3rd post is illustration/pattern, alternating photo posts
- **Stories:** Navy background, cream text, coral highlights
- **Hashtags:** #TasteTheSky #SORASushi #SkyHighSushi

### Signage
- Exterior: Dark navy panel, gold raised letters (SORA + 空)
- Interior: Light wood wall with navy-painted kanji mural
- Lighting: Warm spot on logo, dim ambient around

### Staff Uniform
- Navy apron over black shirt
- Small gold **SORA** embroidered on left chest
- Minimal — no huge logos or busy patterns

## 11. Marketing Strategy

### Digital Presence
- **Instagram:** Grid of 2:1 food-to-brand-content ratio, stories featuring daily specials, Reels showing chef preparation
- **Website:** Single-page with menu, reservation, and delivery ordering — all branded navy/cream/gold
- **Google Maps:** Professional photos, menu link, responsive to reviews

### Launch Tactics
- **Soft launch:** Friends & family event, gather content, refine operations
- **Grand opening:** "Taste the sky" campaign — first 50 guests get a free SORA appetizer
- **Influencer:** 3-5 local food bloggers for Instagram/TikTok coverage
- **Referral:** "Bring a friend" card — both get 10% off next visit

### Takeout / Delivery
- Packaging IS marketing — the navy box with gold foil is your billboard
- Insert a "try this next" card with each order
- Sticker seal on boxes: QR to leave a review + get 5% off

### Seasonal Promotions
| Season | Campaign |
|---|---|
| Spring | Sakura Roll — limited cherry blossom themed roll |
| Summer | SORA Sunset — evening omakase with drink pairing |
| Autumn | Harvest Roll — local seasonal vegetables |
| Winter | Hot SORA — warm sake + comfort rolls |

## 13. Brand Checklist

Before launching any asset, verify:

- [ ] Logo has proper clear space
- [ ] Colors match hex values exactly
- [ ] Typography hierarchy respected
- [ ] Photography is warm-lit, not clinical
- [ ] Copy is short, confident, no jargon
- [ ] No stock clichés (geishas, dragons, Mt Fuji)
- [ ] All applications feel like one brand
